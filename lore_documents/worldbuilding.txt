At the dawn of existence, the world was born from the Primordial Flow, where light and darkness, order and chaos were bound together. Wherever the Flow condensed, names emerged—and different peoples across time and geography gave them different designations: the Forge of Olympus, the roots of Yggdrasil, the peaks of Kunlun, the River of Duat, the Sun of Teotihuacan, the mists of Tír na nÓg. Every mythology is, in truth, a fragmentary record of the same phenomena—different names for the same origin.

From the Flow arose beings who called themselves “gods.” Yet even they could not escape the law of the Flow: any vast power must inevitably leak back toward balance. Thus, the gods divided. The creators scattered seeds of life and civilization, storing power in the coordinates of the constellations. The destroyers sought to devour those seeds by summoning distortion. Their clash ignited the War of Ten Thousand Gods.

In its aftermath, the gods did not vanish. Their essence became Seals and Relics scattered across the world, leaving only echoes in the human memory—what we now call mythology. But as ages passed, the seals weakened, and distortion seeped back through the subterranean divine veins. Whenever the constellations in the night sky tremble, a rift opens, rewriting the land in the logic of a specific myth: the waters of Styx altering rivers, the rainbow bridge of Bifröst bending city walls, the Four Beasts of the East warping terrain.

In these moments, the Guardians of the Constellations awaken. They are inheritors of the Annals of the Gods, wielding the secret craft of binding disparate myths together through Mythic Equivalence Alchemy. Their purpose is simple: to defend the star-altars, restore the proper flow of the Primordial Stream, and repel the armies of distortion—Tiamat’s spawn, Jörmungandr’s fragments, Medusa’s awakened statues, Anubis’s wandering soul legions, the fox-spirits of the East.

Victory in each rift reweaves the world. The logic of that mythology is purified and integrated into the Annals; defeat enshrines the distortion into reality, permanently altering the laws of the world. Thus, the world slowly converges toward a single unified myth—or shatters endlessly into fragments.
